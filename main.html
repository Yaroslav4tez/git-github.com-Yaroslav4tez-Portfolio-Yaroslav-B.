Franz Liszt - solo piano works

  • Transcendental Études
    A set of twelve pieces that are both technically demanding and musically profound.
  • Hungarian Rhapsodies
    Nineteen pieces inspired by Hungarian folk themes, with No. 2 being particularly renowned.
  • Années de Pèlerinage (Years of Pilgrimage)
    Three suites reflecting Liszt's travels and experiences in Switzerland and Italy.
  • Liebesträume (Dreams of Love)
    A set of three lyrical pieces, with No. 3 being especially popular.
  • Mephisto Waltzes
    Four pieces based on the Faust legend, showcasing Liszt's dramatic flair.
  • La Campanella
    A technically challenging piece inspired by Paganini's violin concerto.

Johann Sebastian Bach

  1. Capriccio on the Departure of a Beloved Brother, BWV 992
    Composed around 1704. A charming early keyboard piece in several movements.
  2. Toccata and Fugue in D minor, BWV 565
    Composed circa 1707–1710. One of Bach’s most famous organ works, dramatic and powerful.
  3. Brandenburg Concerto No. 1 in F major, BWV 1046
    Composed around 1717–1721. The first of six concertos dedicated to the Margrave of Brandenburg.
  4. Well-Tempered Clavier, Book I, BWV 846–869
    Composed in 1722. A collection of 24 preludes and fugues in all major and minor keys.
  5. Mass in B minor, BWV 232
    Completed in 1749. One of Bach’s last and most monumental sacred works.
